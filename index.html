Light & Darkness
The dungeon is genuinely dark — only what your party carries is lit. You start with 3 torches. When your light goes out, return to the Tinkerer or restock from monster drops.
🔦 Torch — 12-square radius, 10 minutes, warm flickering flame. Buy for 25g; 3% chance to drop from any slain monster.
🫙 Lantern + 🛢️ Lantern Oil — 24-square radius, 15 minutes per oil, steady golden light. Lantern 100g (reusable), Oil 30g (consumed).
✨ Light spell — mage only. 10 MP for an 14-square cool white light lasting 5 minutes.
Press T any time to pick a source. Only one light burns at a time; starting a new one replaces the old. Timers only tick during exploration (combat pauses them).
Stat Bars
Red — Health (HP). When this reaches 0 the character falls.
Green — Stamina (ST). Powers melee and ranged attacks.
Blue — Mana (MP). Powers magic attacks.
Purple — Experience (XP). Fills as your character earns XP; resets each time they level up.
Attacking with insufficient stamina/mana still works, but deals half damage.
Levels & XP
Every character starts at Level 1 and can reach Level 100.
Defeating a monster grants 10 × its level XP, split evenly across all living party members (summons excluded).
Cumulative XP needed for level N is 100 × N² (400 for L2, 900 for L3, 1,600 for L4, …).
On level-up: +12 HP / +12 ST / +12 MP (scaled by class modifiers), and all pools refill to full.
Each class gains per-level scaling (see Classes below) — higher levels grant better damage, stun/crit/dodge chances, and special bonuses.
Combat Rows
Every character is either Front Row (⚔) or Back Row (🏹). Toggle from the personal inventory screen.
Monsters only target front-row characters with melee attacks. However, ranged and spell attacks can reach back-row characters directly.
When the entire front row is down, combat pauses and you must promote a back-row ally forward.
Only Rogues may melee from the back row (stealth). Everyone else is limited to ranged / magic / special abilities from the back.
Initiative & Turn Order
At the start of each combat, every participant — allies and enemies alike — rolls a d12 to determine turn order. Higher rolls act first; ties are broken randomly.
Class bonuses: Rogues, Monks, and Rangers roll at +1 initiative.
Slow types: Golems and Undead (including undead summons and undead enemies) roll at −1 initiative.
The initiative order is fixed for the whole combat — the same sequence repeats every round (dead combatants are simply skipped).
Summons created mid-combat receive their own initiative roll immediately but act on that roll the following round — they skip the current round after being summoned.
Combat Mechanics
Defense = class + species + per-level bonus + armor + trinkets. Every point directly reduces incoming damage.
Melee attacks have a 20% base stun chance; warriors gain +3% per level.
Ranged attacks have a 20% base crit chance; rangers gain +3% per level. Ranged weapons roll +1 min/+1 max damage over other subtypes.
Magic attacks hit all enemies; mages gain +1% stun per level.
Rogue Backstab: 3× stamina for 2× damage plus +10% per rogue level (+100% at L10). Rogues can also attempt to spot and disarm traps.
Equipped weapons add their power to matching attack types.
Equipped armor reduces incoming damage by its blocking value. See Armor Restrictions below.
Equipped shields have a 25% chance to completely block an incoming attack. Shields cannot be used with a ranged weapon equipped, and Mages/Monks/Necromancers/Artificers cannot use shields at all.
Encounter size: combat always pulls in enough adjacent monsters to match your party size (summons included).
Fallen allies are not auto-revived at the end of combat — use a Resurrection Potion to bring them back.
Status Effects
🟢 Poison — ticks damage at the start of each of your rounds for several turns. Spiders and some monsters apply it on hit. A bigger dose refreshes and supersedes a weaker one. Poison from traps persists out of combat and continues ticking every 10 seconds of exploration time. Poisoned characters show a green 🤢 face badge on their HUD portrait.
✨ Stun — the target loses its next action. Warriors, mages (via magic), and trolls can stun on hit. Weapon riders (⚡ Lightning and ❄️ Ice) also stun the target for 1 round when they proc.
⚡ Shocked (Lightning rider) — stunned for 1 round and attack power reduced for several rounds. Enemy deals less damage while shocked.
❄️ Chilled (Ice rider) — stunned for 1 round and defense reduced for several rounds. Enemy takes extra damage while chilled.
🍃 Entangle (druid) — roots an enemy: its defense is reduced, so your party deals extra damage for the duration.
🎶 Bard Song — AOE debuff applied to all enemies: reduces their attack and defense. Multiple songs may be active at once.
Armor & Shield Restrictions
Cloth only: Mage, Necromancer, Monk
Cloth / Leather: Rogue, Druid, Artificer (no chain or plate)
Cloth / Leather / Chain: Ranger, Bard (no plate)
All armor: Warrior, Cleric, Paladin
No shields: Mage, Monk, Necromancer, Artificer. A shield is also forced off whenever you equip a ranged weapon or enable dual-wielding.
Dungeon Levels & Portals
The dungeon has up to 100 floors. Deeper floors spawn tougher monsters that deal more damage and drop more gold.
🔴 Red portal — descends to the next floor. Appears on every level.
🟢 Green portal — ascends to the previous floor. Appears on levels 2 and deeper.
Step through a portal to travel to the next (or previous) dungeon floor.
Exploration
Walk into enemies — or let them walk into you — to begin combat. Monsters in adjacent cells join the fight, scaled to party size.
The Wandering Tinkerer roams the dungeon. Bumping into them opens a shop (no combat).
Rest at any time in safety to recover. Keep food stocked!
⚠️ Traps: invisible pressure plates lurk on some floor tiles. Types are randomised per trap — pit, swinging blade, dart volley, arrow trap, falling rocks, poison spray, acid jet, fire blast, lightning rune, choking gas, ceiling crusher, scything pendulum, snare net, spring spikes. If you have a Rogue in the party, they may spot the trap before you step on it and you can attempt to disarm it — success scales with rogue level. Without a rogue, you trigger the trap and the whole party takes damage. A modal dialog announces every sprung trap, every successful disarm (revealing any hidden treasure), and every failed disarm. Poison-kind traps (darts, poison spray, choking gas) also apply a poison DoT.
Regeneration
All characters slowly regenerate while exploring: +1 HP/min, +3 ST/min, +2 MP/min by default.
Class and species add to these rates. Regen also continues while menus are open, but not during combat.
Food & Hunger
Every non-summoned party member needs to eat. Food is checked every 30 minutes of real exploration time.
When the check fires, the party automatically consumes 1 food from the group inventory if any is available.
If no food is left, hunger escalates through three stages:
🟡 Hungry — minor stat penalty (−1 to all attacks and defense).
🟠 Starving — significant penalty and stamina drain.
🔴 Dying — characters lose HP each minute until they eat or fall.
The hunger badge appears on each character's portrait in the HUD. Buy food at the Tinkerer (50g) or find it as monster loot. Resting also requires food.
Classes
⚔️ Warrior — Front-line tank. +50% HP/ST, no mana. +1 melee, base +1 defense. All armor, shields OK. Per level: +1 melee, +1 defense, +3% stun.
🏹 Ranger — Bowman. +25% HP/ST, -50% mana. +1 ranged. No plate. Shield OK (but not with a bow equipped). Can Summon Woodland Beast (7 MP): wolf, bear, eagle, or pixie. Per level: +1 ranged, +3% crit.
🪄 Mage — Arcane caster. -25% HP/ST, +50% mana. +1 magic, +2 MP regen. Cloth only, no shield. Per level: +1 magic, +1% magic stun.
🗡️ Rogue — Stealthy striker. +100% ST, no mana. +1 melee, +2 ST regen. Cloth or leather armor. Can melee from back row. Backstab: 3× stamina for 2× damage + 10% per rogue level. Can spot and disarm traps. Per level: +1 melee, +1% instakill.
🧘 Monk — Martial artist. Neutral stats (no HP/ST/MP modifier). +1 melee, +1 ST/MP regen. Cloth only, no shield. Melee costs both ST & MP. 50% whirlwind. 40% base dodge (2 ST + 2 MP, melee only) — caps at 95%. All incoming damage is also reduced by the monk's current dodge %, so a L1 monk on a non-dodged hit still takes 60% damage. Per level: +1 melee, +1% whirlwind, +1% dodge.
✨ Cleric — Healer. +25% HP, -50% ST, +25% mana. All armor, shields OK. Heal: 5 MP → 25% max HP. Revive (unlocks at L3): 25 MP brings a fallen ally back at 25% max HP. Per level: +2% heal power.
💀 Necromancer — Dark caster. -50% HP/ST, +100% mana. Cloth only, no shield. Summon Undead (7 MP, combat only): Skeleton at L1, then stronger tiers every 2 levels (zombie → ghoul → spectre → mummy → ghost → vampire → death knight). Magic drains HP to caster and own undead. Per level: +1 magic, +1 drain.
🎶 Bard — Inspiring performer. -20% ST, +20% MP. +1 MP regen. Cloth/leather/chain (no plate); shields OK. Sing Song (7 MP): AOE debuff to all enemies — reduces their attack and defense (rises by +1 every 2 levels). Multiple bard songs can stack. Per level: +1 magic damage.
🌿 Druid — Nature caster. -15% HP/ST, +30% MP. +1 MP regen. Cloth/leather only (no chain or plate); shields OK. Entangle (8 MP): rolls 50% on each enemy to debuff -2 defense & -2 damage (rises by +1 every 2 levels beyond 1). Summon Woodland Beast (7 MP): wolf/bear/eagle/pixie, like the ranger. Per level: +1 magic damage.
🛡️ Paladin — Holy warrior. +25% HP, neutral ST, -25% MP. All armor, shields OK. Smite (5 MP, front row): armor-ignoring holy melee strike. Against undead or demon foes: +2 damage per paladin level AND a 1% + 1%/level chance to instantly purge the foe. Lay On Hands (5 MP): half-strength cleric heal (12.5% max HP base, +1%/level). Per level: +1 melee, +1 defense, +1%/level smite chance, +1% heal.
⚙️ Artificer — Tinker-gunner. Neutral stats. Cloth/leather only (no chain or plate); no shields. Scatter Shot replaces the default ranged attack: primary shot hits at full damage, plus 2 splash shots at 50% damage (+1 splash every 5 artificer levels). Can forge persistent Golems (Flesh / Clay / Stone / Iron — unlocked at L1/L3/L6/L10) and Repair Golem (1 matching reagent → 50% max HP). Golems survive between combats but permadeath on KO. Open Crafting with K to forge, enchant, brew, or dismiss a golem. Per level: +1 ranged.
Monster Tags
Some monsters carry tags that unlock class interactions. Mouse over an enemy card in combat to see its tag line.
🧟 Undead — vulnerable to paladin Smite (bonus damage & instakill chance) and necromancer life-drain rules.
😈 Demon — also vulnerable to paladin Smite bonus damage and instakill chance.
🐺 Beast — wild creatures summoned or encountered in the dungeon.
🧠 Aberration — eldritch horrors with unusual resistances or abilities.
🐍 Venomous — can apply poison on hit.
🔥 Fireborn — fire-attuned monsters; may deal burn damage.
🦪 Construct — magical constructs (golems, animated objects); immune to poison and some debuffs.
Tags are listed in the enemy tooltip and appear on the Lore Book (X key).
Crafting (K)
Press K while exploring to open the Crafting menu (requires an Artificer in the party). Three tabs: Enchant, Potions, Golems.
Reagents come in three tiers — Common / Uncommon / Rare — dropping from slain monsters with rarer reagents deeper in the dungeon. Reagents are the universal crafting currency for enchanting, brewing, and golem work.
Weapon Enchants — upgrade any equipped weapon to +1 / +2 / +3 (each step flat +damage). After reaching +1 you may attune a single elemental rider (25% proc on melee/ranged hits):
  • 🔥 Fire — 1.5× extra burn DoT for 1 additional round.
  • 🧪 Acid — DoT plus enemy defense debuff.
  • 🟢 Poison — DoT plus enemy damage debuff.
  • ⚡ Lightning — 1-round stun + attack (damage) debuff.
  • ❄️ Ice — 1-round stun + defense debuff.
Armor Enchants — +1 / +2 / +3 to an equipped armor piece (flat bonus defense). Unequipping clears the enchant.
Potions:
  • 🧪 Minor Healing Potion — restores a portion of max HP.
  • 🧪 Greater Healing Potion — restores more HP.
  • 🛡️ Elixir of Warding — timed defense buff (wall-clock; persists across combats until it expires).
  • ⚔️ Elixir of Wrath — timed damage buff (same timing rules).
Golems:
  • 🧟 Flesh Golem (L1, common reagents) — regenerates HP each round.
  • 🧱 Clay Golem (L3, uncommon) — reflects a fraction of incoming damage.
  • 🪨 Stone Golem (L6, uncommon+rare) — periodic area-stun slam.
  • ⚙️ Iron Golem (L10, rare) — cleaves multiple foes, immune to some effects.
Only one golem per artificer at a time. Golems are persistent (they travel with you between combats and floors) but die permanently on KO — no revive, no resurrect. The artificer may Repair Golem in or out of combat (spends 1 reagent of the golem's tier → 50% max HP).
Monster Drops
Every slain monster rolls independently for each loot kind — gold, food, healing potion, weapon/armor/shield/trinket drops — as well as:
✨ Magical Reagents — Common / Uncommon / Rare tiers drop from slain monsters (rarer reagents weight deeper in the dungeon). Reagents power the Crafting menu (K): enchants, potions, and golem forging/repair.
Trinkets & Equipment
Each non-summoned character has five extra equipment slots: Cloak, Neck, Ring I, Ring II, and Belt.
Trinkets come in four bonus types — defense / melee / ranged / magic — across four tiers (Common, Uncommon, Rare, Legendary) for +1 to +4.
Trinkets are rare loot drops from defeated monsters (2% base chance, scaling with dungeon depth). The Tinkerer stocks a small rotating selection of lower-tier trinkets.
Rings can be worn in either ring slot; cloaks/necks/belts each have a dedicated slot.
Summons
🐺 Wolf / 🐻 Bear: heavy melee with stun. 🦅 Eagle: ranged crits. 🧚 Pixie: magic AoE.
🦴 Necromancer undead start at Skeleton (L1), then every 2 levels unlock: zombie → ghoul → spectre → mummy → ghost → vampire → death knight. Each new tier grants +50% HP, +2 damage, +1 defense.
Ranger & Druid beasts can be healed by clerics. Necromancer undead cannot — but the necromancer's drain restores them.
Artificer Golems are a separate summon family — persistent, one per artificer, permadeath on KO. Only the forging artificer may repair them (1 tier-matched reagent for 50% HP) and they survive between combats.
Summons appear in the party roster during combat (and can be targeted & healed), and are removed from the roster when combat ends — except for persistent golems, which remain in the roster until they fall.
Species
👤 Human — Well-rounded. +1 HP regen, +1 ST regen.
🧝 Elf — Graceful archer-mages. +1 ranged, +1 magic.
⛏️ Dwarf — Stout mountain-folk. +1 melee, +1 ST regen.
👹 Orc — Brutish warriors. +1 melee, +1 HP regen.
🧙 Gnome — Tinkering illusionists. +1 magic, +1 MP regen.
🧑 Halfling — Nimble wanderers. +1 defense, +1 ST regen.
Items & Shop
🍎 Food (50g) — consumed when resting.
🧪 Healing Potion (50g) — restores full HP to one character.
🕯️ Resurrection Potion (200g) — revives a fallen party member to full HP/ST/MP.
Artificer-crafted potions (see Crafting): Minor / Greater Healing, Elixir of Warding (timed defense), Elixir of Wrath (timed damage). Elixirs tick by wall-clock and persist through combat boundaries until they expire.
Weapons, armor, and shields are sold at the Wandering Tinkerer (50g per power/blocking, 150g shields). Sell any item at half price.
🧣 Trinkets — cloak/neck/ring/belt accessories. Tier 1 available at the shop (150g × tier), higher tiers drop from monsters.
